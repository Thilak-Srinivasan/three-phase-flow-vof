Thilak S

Multiphysics Simulation · CFD · AI · Sustainable Design

I am a fourth-year Integrated Dual Degree student at BITS Pilani, pursuing an M.Sc. in Mathematics and a B.E. in Mechanical Engineering. I work at the nexus of multiphysics simulation, AI, and sustainable design — contributing to research on biomedical devices, thermal systems, and predictive maintenance.

I am moving beyond "black-box" simulations by building a 2D Compressible Navier–Stokes solver from scratch using the Rusanov Finite Volume Method, simulating oblique shock-wave interactions to understand the underlying physics of fluid engineering. My goal is to develop transferable methodologies and validated models that drive scientific understanding and real-world impact.
